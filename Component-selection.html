Individual Component Selection

This page documents my individual component selection including considered options for each "role" as well as the logic behind my hardware choice. Due to project requirements, through-hole components will not be considered for selection.

System functionality

Our team's project is a simulated wind-tunnel, where we use a small airplane on a pivot with streamers to show airflow, as well as a 7-segment display reflecting windspeed. These values can be controlled by an operator using dials. My part of the project is the human interfact, the part of the exhibit that the viewer will directly interact with. It will use 2 dials (potentiometers with a knobs fit over the shafts) to send take input from the user, then send that information to the other systems downstream.

Power Regulator Selection

For my power regulator selection, I am looking to minimize the number of required support components, and simplify the circuit as much as possible. This way, debugging will be much easier because there are less areas where something could go wrong.

My Selection

Component Image Justification
AP63200WU-7 The main draw of this regulator is its maximum imput voltage. It has a high enough maximum input voltage that it will be able to handle being connected to the power supply used by the other systems, simplifying the master system's design.
Potentiometers

The main function of this system is to read the values from 2 potentiometers, then send that information downstream to my teammate's systems. The potentiometers are also the component of the exhibit that the user will interact with. It is critical that I find a good potentiometer.

Component Image Advantages Disadvantages Hyperlink
MH22B-10-1K
  • Good size
  • Designed for panel mount
  • Dial shaft is good shape for creating a custom knob to fit over this one
  • very expensive
  • technically not surface mount
RK1191124001
  • affordable price
  • workable dial shaft
  • Small size
  • May be difficult to position inside exhibit due to it being surface mount

My Selection

Component Image Justification
RK1191124001 Surface mount potentiometers that have shafts are surprisingly difficult to find, especially at a good price. This one checks the boxes for what I need. It is surface mount, it has a shaft that can have a knob made for it, and it can connect to my microcontroller.
Analog Reading IC

To read the potentiometers, the readings coming from the potentiometer will be fed through an analog-to-digital converter, which will then send the digital signal to the microcontroller.

  • low cost
  • simple connections
  • can connect to multiple potentiometers
  • Complicated extra functions
  • May interfere with reading
ADC081C021
  • Only 6 pins
  • Lowest cost option
  • Very simple
  • I2C
  • Need one for each potentiometer
  • Need multiple connections to microcontroller

My Selection

Component Image Justification
ADC081C021 This ADC is the simplest solution for what I need it to do. It's low cost, simple to set up, and easier to debug.
